Rules & Regulations

Understand the guidelines, eligibility criteria, and competition framework to ensure a fair and successful experience.

Eligibility Requirements

Participation is open to undergraduate and graduate students from accredited universities worldwide. Teams must consist of 3-4 members with diverse academic backgrounds and skill sets. All participants must be currently enrolled students and cannot have graduated before the competition start date.

  • Teams must have 3-4 members from the same or different universities
  • At least one member must be an undergraduate student
  • All members must be currently enrolled in an accredited institution
  • No prior professional consulting experience required
  • International teams are welcome and encouraged

Competition Format

The competition follows a structured case study format with clear phases and deliverables. Teams will receive comprehensive case materials and have dedicated time for analysis, strategy development, and presentation preparation. The format ensures fair evaluation while allowing creativity and strategic thinking.

  • 48-hour case analysis period with dedicated mentorship sessions
  • Access to proprietary datasets and market research
  • Weekly office hours with industry professionals
  • Final presentation format: 15-20 slides with 10-minute Q&A
  • Submission deadline: December 1st, 2025 at 11:59 PM PST

Submission Guidelines

All submissions must follow specific formatting requirements and include comprehensive analysis with supporting materials. Teams must demonstrate thorough research, strategic thinking, and practical implementation plans. Submissions will be evaluated based on clarity, innovation, and feasibility.

  • Executive presentation: 15-20 slides maximum
  • Detailed financial model with 3-year projections
  • Market analysis and competitive landscape assessment
  • Implementation roadmap with key milestones
  • Risk assessment and mitigation strategies
  • All materials must be submitted as PDF documents

Judging Criteria

A panel of industry experts from top consulting firms and technology companies will evaluate submissions based on comprehensive criteria. The judging process ensures fair assessment while recognizing innovation, strategic thinking, and practical applicability of proposed solutions.

  • Strategic insight and market understanding (25%)
  • Innovation and creative problem-solving (25%)
  • Financial feasibility and business viability (20%)
  • Implementation plan and execution strategy (20%)
  • Presentation quality and communication (10%)
  • Judges include senior executives from McKinsey, BCG, and Aurora Labs
